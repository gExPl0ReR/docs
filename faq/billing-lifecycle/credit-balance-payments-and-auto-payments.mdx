# Credit Balance Payments and Auto Payments

This page explains how account credit works and how to set up automated payments.

---

## How to Pay Invoices with Account Credit

Credit is applied automatically to **future invoices**.

If account credit is topped up **after an invoice has already been created**, that invoice must be paid **manually**.

## Setting up a Fully Automated Payment Workflow

Below is an overview of available payment methods and how well they work for automation.

When you pay an invoice with your credit card, your card is safely stored with our payment provider Stripe.

A reference token is stored in your account, which allows us to securely debit your card exactly when an invoice is due.

Renewal invoices are charged automatically.  
New orders are also charged automatically and provisioned in near real-time.

Top up your account to ensure renewal invoices are automatically funded from your account balance.

Account credit can be funded using credit cards, PayPal, direct debit, or cryptocurrency payments.

### PayPal Subscriptions ğŸ¥‡

PayPal subscriptions work well for private use cases with a limited number of services.

For business use cases involving frequent upgrades, cancellations, or re-orders, PayPal subscriptions can become difficult to manage.

In such cases, credit card payments or account credit are recommended.

When transitioning away from PayPal subscriptions:
- Cancel existing subscriptions in your PayPal account
- Monitor renewal invoices during the transition
- Pay invoices manually if needed to avoid service suspension

Your Support PIN helps us verify account ownership when processing sensitive VPS-related requests such as bespoke reinstalls, manual IP changes, or account modifications.

## Where can you find your Support PIN?

Your Support PIN is included in the VPS credentials email sent immediately after your order was placed.

Look for an email with one of the following subjects:

- **EDIS Global: Your Linux VPS Credentials**
- **EDIS Global: Your Windows VPS Credentials**

You can find the Support PIN in the **Plan details** section of the email, directly below your **KVM ID**.

## Support PIN in the control panel

You can also find your Support PIN directly in the VPS control panel.

### Steps

1. Log in to the VPS Control Panel.
2. Open the **Services** section.
3. Select your VPS instance.
4. Locate the **Information** panel.

Your Support PIN is displayed at the bottom of the information window.

![Support PIN](/assets/vps-support-pin.webp)

## When do you need the Support PIN?

You may be asked to provide your Support PIN when:

- Contacting support regarding your VPS
- Requesting sensitive changes
- Authorizing reinstalls or IP changes
- Verifying account ownership

## Security recommendations

- Keep your Support PIN confidential
- Do not share it publicly or with unauthorized users
- Only provide it directly to EDIS Global support staff when requested

## Need help?

If you cannot locate your Support PIN, please contact EDIS Global Support through Messenger, Telegram, WhatsApp, or the support portal.
